# Whole-Book Quality Checklist

## Educational quality

- [ ] Each chapter states what the reader will learn.
- [ ] Concepts are introduced before they are relied upon.
- [ ] Plain-language explanation precedes formal terminology.
- [ ] Examples become progressively more complex.
- [ ] Exercises test application, not only recall.

## Technical accuracy

- [ ] Framework names and versions are correct.
- [ ] Related notations are compared accurately.
- [ ] Logical and physical architecture are not conflated.
- [ ] BIAN concepts are represented as reference concepts, not implementation mandates.
- [ ] Current claims are sourced and dated.

## Consistency

- [ ] Terms match `GLOSSARY.md`.
- [ ] Case-study names and facts are stable.
- [ ] Figure IDs are unique.
- [ ] Chapter numbering and cross-references are valid.
- [ ] British spelling and punctuation rules are followed.

## Diagrams

- [ ] Every diagram has a stated purpose and audience.
- [ ] Boundaries, responsibilities and important relationships are clear.
- [ ] Colour is not the only carrier of meaning.
- [ ] Editable source and SVG export exist.
- [ ] Captions explain intent and limitations.

## Sources and rights

- [ ] Major claims have authoritative sources.
- [ ] Source notes record version and access date.
- [ ] Quotations are limited and attributed.
- [ ] Diagrams are original or have a recorded licence.
- [ ] Final references are complete.

## Publication readiness

- [ ] All chapters are approved.
- [ ] Scripts pass.
- [ ] Combined manuscript builds.
- [ ] Front matter and appendices are complete.
- [ ] No critical review findings remain.
