# Chapter 1 Architecture Modelling Design

## Purpose

Complete Chapter 1, **What Is Architecture Modelling?**, as the first reader-facing chapter of the book, using Chapter 5 as the approved structural and writing model.

## Scope

Chapter 1 introduces the basic reason architecture modelling exists. It explains that architecture models are purposeful simplifications used to communicate, analyse options and make decisions. It defines the opening vocabulary needed by later chapters: architecture, model, diagram, view, viewpoint, notation and framework.

The chapter will use:

- A simple online store for the first example.
- Horizon Bank for the enterprise example.
- One conceptual figure, `FIG-01-01`, to distinguish reality, model and views.

## Out of Scope

Chapter 1 will not teach any major notation in detail. Chapter 2 will deepen model, view and viewpoint. Later chapters will teach UML, C4, BPMN, ArchiMate, data, decisions, domain events, infrastructure and security.

## Sources

Use `[ISO-42010]` for architecture-description vocabulary. The chapter should paraphrase rather than quote the standard. Source details are recorded under `research/fundamentals/` and `SOURCE_REGISTER.md`.
