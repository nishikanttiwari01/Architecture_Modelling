# FIG-32-01: Full-service bank operating model

## Purpose

Connect customer segments and product families to front, middle and back office responsibilities, enterprise control and accounting.

## Audience

Readers with software experience and limited banking experience.

## Question answered

Why does a full-service bank need distinct channel, processing, risk, finance, integration and data responsibilities?

## Notation

Draw.io layered explanatory view after author approval.

## Required elements

Customer segments, channels, business lines, front office, middle office, back office, risk and compliance, finance, shared services, legal entities and external infrastructures.

## Required relationships

Label service, control, settlement, accounting and reporting flows.

## Main flow or structure

Customer-to-enterprise front-to-back structure with accounting and control as visible responsibilities.

## Alternative and exception flows

Include assisted service and exception repair.

## Scope

Conceptual whole-bank model.

## Exclusions

Detailed organisation chart, application products and jurisdiction-specific obligations.

## Accessibility requirements

Landscape layout; text group labels; patterns or borders supplement colour.

## Review criteria

All major business lines and enterprise functions are visible; accounting, controls and exceptions are not omitted.
