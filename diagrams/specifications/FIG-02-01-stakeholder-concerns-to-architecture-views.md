# FIG-02-01: From Stakeholder Concerns to Architecture Views

## Purpose

Show how stakeholder concerns lead to architecture views through a viewpoint, and how model kinds, notation, architecture models and correspondences support those views.

## Audience

Beginners reading Chapter 2, plus architects who need a concise reminder of the ISO 42010 vocabulary used in the chapter.

## Question answered

How do stakeholders, concerns, viewpoints, views, architecture models, model kinds and correspondences fit together in practical architecture modelling?

## Notation

Conceptual PlantUML illustration.

## Required elements

- Stakeholders
- Concerns
- Architecture viewpoint
- Architecture view
- Architecture models
- Notation or model kinds
- Related architecture view
- Correspondence between related views

## Required relationships

- Stakeholders raise concerns.
- Concerns are addressed by a viewpoint.
- The viewpoint guides construction, interpretation, analysis and review of the view.
- Model kinds or notation shape the architecture models used in the view.
- The architecture view contains or uses architecture models.
- Related views are checked through correspondences.

## Main flow or structure

Show a left-to-right flow from stakeholders to concerns, then to a viewpoint, then to an architecture view. Show model kinds or notation feeding the architecture models used by the view. Show a second related view and a correspondence between the two views.
