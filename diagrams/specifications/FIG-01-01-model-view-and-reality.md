# FIG-01-01: Model, view and reality

## Purpose

Show that an architecture model is a purposeful simplification of reality and that different views can be created from the same model for different audiences.

## Audience

Beginners reading Chapter 1.

## Question answered

How are the real organisation, an architecture model and reader-facing views related?

## Notation

Conceptual PlantUML illustration.

## Required elements

- Real situation
- Architecture model
- Business view
- Software view
- Deployment view
- Decision or action

## Required relationships

- The model selects relevant facts from the real situation.
- Views are created from the model for different questions.
- Views inform decisions and action.
- Decisions and action affect the real situation.

## Main flow or structure

Show a left-to-right explanation from reality to model to views to decision, with a feedback relationship from decision back to reality.

## Alternative and exception flows

None.

## Scope

Conceptual explanation only.

## Exclusions

No formal UML, C4, BPMN, ArchiMate or BIAN notation. No detailed system structure.

## Accessibility requirements

Use concise labels, strong contrast and relationship labels. Do not rely on colour alone.

## Review criteria

- The diagram distinguishes model from diagram/view.
- It is readable at book-page width.
- It does not introduce notation-specific symbols before the reader has learned them.
