# Horizon Bank Reconciliation Catalogue

This catalogue defines comparison points, expected evidence and ownership of unresolved differences for the fictional Horizon Bank. A reconciliation detects or explains a difference. It does not, by itself, authorise an accounting correction, customer adjustment or external resubmission. Each exception therefore remains with the named exception owner until it is resolved, accepted under a governed policy or escalated to the relevant control owner.

`Compared Record A` and `Compared Record B` name the two governed populations or balances being compared. Application and interface identifiers remain gaps until those catalogues are integrated. No row asserts a reconciliation frequency, materiality threshold, recovery objective or regulatory requirement.

## Governance rules

- A reconciliation record must identify both compared populations, an accountable control owner and a separate operational exception owner where the responsibilities differ.
- A break may be corrected only through the governed product, accounting, case or operational process. The reconciliation record is evidence of comparison, not correction authority.
- Timeliness, tolerance, matching keys, materiality, ageing, attestation, evidence retention and escalation are governed implementation details. They remain gaps until approved for the relevant legal entity and jurisdiction.
- External statements and confirmations require explicit external-network and interface relationships when those catalogues are integrated.
- Application and system-of-record relationships must state which attributes and lifecycle states are authoritative. A generic application name is not sufficient.
