# Horizon Bank Interface Catalogue

This author-model catalogue records logical exchanges, not physical endpoints or vendor protocols. For an `API`, the producer is the application that provides the operation and the consumer invokes it. For a `Command`, `Event`, `Message`, `Batch`, `File` or `Workflow` hand-off, the producer emits or initiates the exchange and the consumer receives or acts on it.

## Interface rules

- Interface type is limited to `API`, `Command`, `Event`, `Message`, `Batch`, `File` or `Workflow`.
- An external-network relationship identifies a logical connectivity boundary. It does not assert that Horizon Bank is a member of, certified for or directly connected to a named network.
- Security classifications are author-model handling categories and require alignment with the bank's governed information-classification policy.
- Operational transport, endpoint, protocol, version, service-level and recovery details remain separate design concerns.
