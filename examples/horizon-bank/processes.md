# Horizon Bank Process Catalogue

This catalogue defines the fictional bank's governed Level 1 and Level 2 processes. A process describes how work progresses from a trigger to an outcome. It is not a capability, organisation unit, application boundary or Banking Industry Architecture Network (BIAN) Service Domain.

The retained `HB-PROC-01` to `HB-PROC-06` identifiers now serve as Level 1 process families. Additional Level 1 records close whole-bank coverage gaps. Level 2 records provide a stable decomposition aligned to the business-domain and capability catalogues. Relationships express traceability and contribution, not equivalence.

## Relationship and hierarchy rules

- `Parent ID` is decomposition. Every Level 2 process has exactly one Level 1 parent.
- `Relationships` records contribution and traceability to governed domains, capabilities, organisations, value streams and parent processes. It does not make those concepts equivalent.
- Level 1 process families provide whole-bank navigation. Level 2 records identify accountable end-to-end management flows. Product, channel, jurisdiction and exception-specific Level 3 procedures are deliberately deferred until their variants are governed.
- A capability describes an ability and can support several processes. A process describes ordered work from a trigger to an outcome and can use several capabilities.
