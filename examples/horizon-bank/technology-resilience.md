# Horizon Bank Technology and Resilience Classification Catalogue

This catalogue assigns governed logical classifications to the fictional Horizon Bank technology estate. A classification guides dependency analysis, recovery design, operational ownership, observability and testing. It is not a service-level agreement and does not invent a Recovery Time Objective (RTO), Recovery Point Objective (RPO), impact tolerance, transaction volume or infrastructure topology.

## Local classification vocabularies

The permitted `Technology Class` values are:

- `Customer interaction`: technology used directly by customers, employees or partners to initiate or service activity.
- `Financial transaction processing`: technology that records, controls, routes or settles financial transactions and positions.
- `Business record and workflow`: technology that manages governed business records, documents, cases, decisions or work.
- `Control and decisioning`: technology that performs risk, compliance, fraud, authorisation or other governed decisions and controls.
- `Integration and external connectivity`: technology that transports, adapts, schedules or governs exchanges within or beyond the bank.
- `Data, accounting and reporting`: technology that maintains accounting, analytical, lineage, reporting or authoritative data responsibilities.
- `Security and technology control plane`: technology that secures, observes, configures or operates the estate.
- `Enterprise support`: technology supporting workforce and corporate administration rather than direct banking processing.

The permitted `Resilience Class` values are:

- `Critical operation direct`: directly performs an activity identified as a critical-operation candidate.
- `Critical operation supporting`: provides a dependency required by a critical-operation candidate but is not the customer outcome itself.
- `Time-sensitive financial processing`: supports financial processing where delayed or duplicated work can affect settlement, positions, customer funds or financial records.
- `Controlled business service`: supports a governed business, risk, compliance, reporting or evidence lifecycle with recoverable manual or delayed modes to be defined.
- `Enterprise support`: supports internal administration with business continuity and recovery needs to be determined through impact analysis.

## Classification and governance rules

- `Resilience Class` expresses the kind of consequence and continuity concern. It does not set an RTO, RPO, impact tolerance or recovery priority.
- Business impact analysis must set service-level tolerances with the accountable business owner before a physical recovery design is claimed to satisfy them.
- Applications may inherit a classification only through an explicit `HB-APP-*` relationship. Shared platforms can support several classifications, and the strictest dependency must not be inferred without scenario analysis.
- Every critical operation requires an end-to-end dependency view covering people, premises, applications, data, interfaces, external networks, security services and operational procedures.
- Recovery design must cover failover, data integrity, reconciliation, failback and evidence. Redundant infrastructure alone is not evidence of resilience.
- Current, transition and target implementations may have different classifications or gaps. Classification records do not conceal those architecture-state differences.
