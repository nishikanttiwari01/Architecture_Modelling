# Horizon Bank Organisation and Role Catalogue

This catalogue defines the fictional organisation units and accountable roles used by the Horizon Bank case study. Organisation structure records where work and accountability sit. They do not define process sequence, application boundaries or Banking Industry Architecture Network (BIAN) Service Domains.

Level 1 records are group functions or governing bodies. Level 2 records are accountable business or functional units and roles reporting directly to a Level 1 function. Level 3 role records sit within a Level 2 unit. Relationships provide governed traceability to business domains, capabilities, processes, business lines and value streams without asserting equivalence.

## Boundary rules

- Business units own propositions and customer or financial outcomes. Operations units perform controlled fulfilment and repair but do not automatically own product policy.
- Enterprise Risk and Compliance provide independent oversight. Internal Audit remains structurally independent of both first-line operations and second-line control functions.
- A role record captures accountability, not a person. The fictional bank does not name individual office holders.
- Organisation hierarchy records reporting and accountability context. Process, capability, application and BIAN boundaries require separate governance decisions.
