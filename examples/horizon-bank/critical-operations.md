# Horizon Bank Critical-Operation Catalogue

This catalogue identifies candidate customer, market and bank outcomes whose disruption could cause significant harm in the fictional Horizon Bank. It is an author model for dependency and resilience analysis. It does not assert regulatory classification, an impact tolerance, a Recovery Time Objective (RTO), a Recovery Point Objective (RPO) or a service-level agreement.

`Degraded-Mode Assumption` records a design question, not an approved workaround. Every candidate needs business impact analysis, jurisdiction assessment and end-to-end testing across people, premises, applications, information, interfaces, external networks and suppliers.

## Governance rules

- A critical operation is an end-to-end outcome, not the name of one application, team or infrastructure component.
- Proposed criticality and degraded-mode assumptions require accountable business approval and jurisdiction assessment.
- Recovery design must include data integrity, replay or restart boundaries, reconciliation, customer communication, operational capacity and failback.
- A technology classification supports dependency analysis but cannot set an impact tolerance, RTO or RPO on behalf of the business owner.
