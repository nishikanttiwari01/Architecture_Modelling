# Horizon Bank Scenario Catalogue

This catalogue defines integrated, end-to-end scenarios for the fictional Horizon Bank. A scenario traces a business trigger to an observable outcome across people, applications, information, interfaces, accounting, controls, reconciliation and resilience. It is not a detailed Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) process or a claim that every path is straight through.

The relationships provide a governed starting set, not an exhaustive dependency list. Detailed variants, exceptions, service levels and sequence diagrams remain chapter and scenario design work.

## Governance rules

- Every scenario must have one trigger, one observable outcome and one accountable business owner.
- A scenario relationship shows participation or dependency. It does not assert that a BIAN Service Domain equals an application, software service, team or database.
- Detailed human work, gateways and exceptions belong in a BPMN process when that view is required. Detailed runtime order belongs in a sequence or dynamic view.
- Scenario completion requires explicit exception, accounting, reconciliation, control, resilience and customer-communication outcomes where they apply.
