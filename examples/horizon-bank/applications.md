# Horizon Bank Logical Application Catalogue

This catalogue is an author model of logical application responsibilities for a fictional full-service bank. A logical application can be implemented by one package, several deployable services or a retained platform. It is not a vendor inventory, deployment topology or claim that Horizon Bank operates a particular product.

`Data Authority Role` states the application's governed data responsibility. `System-of-Record Relationships` refers to an authority decision, not automatic ownership of every copy of the data. Resilience classes are working classifications that require alignment to the technology and resilience catalogue.

## Boundary rules

- An application ID identifies a logical responsibility boundary, not a deployable service, team, database or BIAN Service Domain.
- A system-of-record relationship is qualified by information purpose and lifecycle. Replicas, analytical copies and integration platforms are not made authoritative merely because they store data.
- `Tier 1` to `Tier 3` are working resilience classes, not recovery-time or availability promises. The technology and resilience catalogue must govern their final definitions.
- A blank system-of-record relationship means that the application is not assigned to one of the currently governed authority decisions. It does not mean that the application stores no data.
