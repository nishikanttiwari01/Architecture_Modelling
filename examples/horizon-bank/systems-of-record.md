# Horizon Bank System-of-Record Catalogue

This catalogue records qualified authority decisions for the fictional Horizon Bank. Authority is always limited by information purpose, lifecycle stage, legal entity and effective time. A logical application can be authoritative for one attribute group and only a consumer or working copy for another.

`Authority Conditions` makes the boundary explicit. The application relationship identifies the logical responsibility, not a vendor product or physical database.

## Governance rules

- A system-of-record assignment must name the information purpose, lifecycle stage, legal entity and effective time for which it is authoritative.
- Authoritative does not mean sole copy. Operational caches, analytical copies and external records require lineage and reconciliation where they affect decisions.
- A workflow or reporting application is not automatically authoritative for the business facts it consumes.
- Corrections must be made through the governed authority for the affected fact. A reconciliation, data platform or report must not silently overwrite source records.
