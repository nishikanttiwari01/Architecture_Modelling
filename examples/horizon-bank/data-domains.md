# Horizon Bank Data-Domain Catalogue

This catalogue is an author model of the major information domains required by a fictional full-service bank. A data domain groups information by business meaning and accountability. It is not a database, an application boundary or a claim that one system owns every attribute throughout its lifecycle.

`Key Information Concepts` gives representative concepts for scope control. Authority is assigned separately in the system-of-record catalogue by purpose, lifecycle stage, legal entity and effective time.

## Governance rules

- A data domain controls meaning, ownership and policy. It does not imply one physical data store or one application.
- `Sensitive`, `highly sensitive` and other descriptive classifications in this catalogue are scope labels for the author model, not approved policy classifications.
- Every authoritative assignment must state the purpose, lifecycle stage, legal entity and effective time in `systems-of-record.md`.
- Derived analytical data must retain lineage to its governed sources. A reporting platform does not become authoritative for the originating business transaction merely because it stores a copy.
