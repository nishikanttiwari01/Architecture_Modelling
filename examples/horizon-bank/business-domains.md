# Horizon Bank Business-Domain Catalogue

This catalogue defines the governed business areas used to organise Horizon Bank's architecture. A business domain groups related responsibilities and knowledge. It is not an organisation chart, process sequence, application boundary or Banking Industry Architecture Network (BIAN) Service Domain. The catalogue is an author model for the fictional bank.

Level 1 records provide whole-bank navigation. Level 2 records define coherent subdomains. A Level 2 record has exactly one Level 1 parent. Relationships point to governed business lines, products, value streams, capabilities, processes or organisation records without asserting equivalence.

## Relationship rules

- `Parent ID` records decomposition only. It never represents contribution, dependency or application support.
- `Relationships` records explicit traceability to governed Horizon Bank records. It does not imply that a business domain is identical to a capability, value stream, process, organisation unit or BIAN Service Domain.
- Level 1 domains provide navigation and must not be counted again when their Level 2 children are used for coverage analysis.
- A business responsibility that crosses several domains is represented by relationships, not by giving a subdomain several parents.
