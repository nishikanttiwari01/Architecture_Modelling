# Horizon Bank Capability Catalogue

This catalogue defines what Horizon Bank must be able to do. A capability is a stable business ability, not a process step, organisation unit, application, project or Banking Industry Architecture Network (BIAN) Service Domain. The hierarchy is an author model for the fictional bank.

Level 1 records form the enterprise capability map. Level 2 records are mutually understandable children used by domain chapters and the coverage matrix. A child has exactly one parent. Relationships show contribution and traceability, not equivalence.

## Relationship rules

- `Parent ID` means decomposition. A Level 2 capability is counted within its Level 1 parent and must not also be counted as a separate Level 1 ability.
- `Relationships` records contribution and traceability. It does not assert that a capability is identical to a business domain, value stream, process, product, organisation unit, application or BIAN Service Domain.
- A capability may support several value streams and processes. That reuse is recorded through explicit relationships or the master coverage matrix, not by giving the capability several parents.
- Application enablement belongs in the application catalogue and coverage matrix. Application names must not be substituted for capability names here.
