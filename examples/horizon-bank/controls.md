# Horizon Bank Control Catalogue

This catalogue is an author model of preventive, detective and corrective controls for a fictional full-service bank. It records control intent, ownership, evidence and architecture traceability. It does not claim a jurisdictional obligation, control frequency, operating effectiveness or regulatory compliance.

`Evidence Expectation` names the minimum kind of reviewable evidence. Detailed rules, thresholds, sampling, frequency, segregation and retention require approval for the relevant legal entity and jurisdiction.

## Governance rules

- A control record defines intent and expected evidence. It does not demonstrate that the control operated effectively.
- A control must have one accountable control owner even when several applications, teams or legal entities perform activities.
- Automated controls require governed rules or models, input completeness, version evidence, exception handling and human override boundaries.
- Corrective activity must use the authorised business, accounting or operational process. A monitoring or reconciliation application must not silently change authoritative records.
