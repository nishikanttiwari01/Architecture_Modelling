## Supported claims

- Claim: A lightweight Architecture Decision Record can preserve an architecturally significant choice using title, status, context, decision and consequences, while later records may supersede earlier ones.
- Intended chapter(s): Chapter 23
- Normative or interpretive: Interpretive practitioner pattern, not a formal standard

## Relevant summary

Nygard proposes concise records for architecturally significant decisions. The record preserves the context, selected response and consequences. Supersession keeps decision history visible rather than silently rewriting it.

## Terminology and version notes

Chapter 23 uses `Architecture Decision Record (ADR)` for this pattern and distinguishes it from repeatable operational decision logic modelled with DMN.
