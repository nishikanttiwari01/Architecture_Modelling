## Supported claims

- Claim: Security and privacy controls can be grouped into families and mapped to architecture concerns, but a control catalogue is not itself an architecture diagram.
- Intended chapter(s): Chapter 12, Chapter 21, Chapter 48
- Normative or interpretive: Official control-catalogue guidance; interpretive for modelling and review guidance.

- Claim: Security control mapping should record control intent, affected assets or flows, responsible owner and evidence rather than only naming a control family.
- Intended chapter(s): Chapter 12, Chapter 21, Chapter 48
- Normative or interpretive: Interpretive modelling guidance based on official control-catalogue structure.

## Relevant summary

NIST SP 800-53 Revision 5 provides a broad catalogue of security and privacy controls. Chapter 12 will use it to explain control mapping and review traceability, not to prescribe a mandatory control baseline for every reader.

## Terminology and version notes

Record the source as Revision 5, update 1. NIST notes a 2025 Release 5.2.0 supplemental minor release on the publication page; do not treat that supplemental release as a new manuscript baseline unless the relevant supplemental material is used. Do not describe SP 800-53 as a universal regulation or as automatically applicable to all organisations.
