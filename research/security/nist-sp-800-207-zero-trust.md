- Intended chapter(s): Chapter 12, Chapter 21, Chapter 48
- Normative or interpretive: Official zero-trust guidance; interpretive for beginner modelling examples.

## Relevant summary

NIST SP 800-207 explains zero trust as an architecture approach that reduces implicit trust and makes access decisions explicit. Chapter 12 will use it to support trust-boundary, identity and authorisation modelling guidance without turning the chapter into a zero-trust implementation manual.

## Terminology and version notes

Use "zero trust" carefully as an architecture approach, not as a product label. Distinguish official NIST terminology from the author's practical diagramming recommendations.
