## Supported claims

- Claim: Tax accounting includes current and deferred tax concerns and cannot be reduced to filing a return.
- Claim: Tax positions require governed source data, calculations, accounting treatment and filing evidence.
- Intended chapter(s): 46
- Normative or interpretive: Official high-level description; the Horizon Bank application and reconciliation design is illustrative.

## Relevant summary

IAS 12 prescribes accounting treatment for income taxes and distinguishes current tax from deferred tax arising from temporary differences, subject to its detailed requirements. The chapter uses only that high-level distinction to explain why a tax architecture needs links from source transactions and ledger balances through calculations and adjustments to recognised positions and submitted filings.

## Terminology and version notes

This note does not establish which tax regimes apply to Horizon Bank. Real implementations require current standards, tax law and jurisdiction-specific advice.
