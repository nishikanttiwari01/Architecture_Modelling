## Supported claims

- Claim: The international anti-money-laundering and counter-terrorist-financing framework is risk based and is implemented through jurisdiction-specific measures.
- Claim: Customer controls, transaction monitoring, investigation, escalation and disclosure are related but distinct responsibilities.
- Intended chapter(s): 47
- Normative or interpretive: Official international standard; Horizon Bank's capability and application separation is an author model.

## Relevant summary

The Recommendations provide an international framework against money laundering, terrorist financing and proliferation financing. Countries adapt the framework to their legal and institutional circumstances. The architecture implication is that obligations, customer risk, screening, monitoring, investigation, decisions and evidence need explicit ownership and versioned jurisdictional rules. The chapter does not claim that a particular control or disclosure path applies in an unspecified country.
