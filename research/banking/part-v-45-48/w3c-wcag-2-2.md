## Relevant summary

WCAG 2.2 is a stable W3C Recommendation for accessible web content. Its success criteria provide a more reviewable basis than statements such as `the channel is accessible`. The chapter uses it to require explicit channel-quality evidence while avoiding claims about which law or conformance level applies to fictional Horizon Bank.

## Terminology and version notes

Define `Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)` on first mention. W3C encourages use of the latest WCAG 2 version. WCAG 2.2 adds criteria while retaining backwards compatibility with earlier WCAG 2 versions, subject to the standard's notes.
