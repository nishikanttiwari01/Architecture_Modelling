- Publication date: 16 July 2024

## Supported claims

- Claim: Interest rate risk in the banking book requires governed measurement assumptions and currency-sensitive shock scenarios.
- Claim: Current prudential calibration can change, so architecture should version models, inputs, parameters and results rather than hard-code an example calibration.
- Intended chapter(s): 45
- Normative or interpretive: Official standard update; the proposed Horizon Bank lineage and application controls are author recommendations.

## Relevant summary

The 2024 publication recalibrated prescribed interest-rate shocks and was incorporated into the consolidated Basel Framework. It illustrates why an asset and liability management architecture must retain the effective version of assumptions and scenario parameters used for each result. The chapter does not copy the calibration or claim a particular jurisdictional implementation.

## Terminology and version notes

Use `interest rate risk in the banking book (IRRBB)` at first mention. The revised standard was due for implementation by 1 January 2026. Readers must check the consolidated Basel Framework and applicable local rules for a real bank.
