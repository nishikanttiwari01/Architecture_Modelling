## Supported claims

- Claim: Operational resilience concerns a bank's ability to deliver critical operations through disruption.
- Claim: Banks should map interconnections and interdependencies, manage third-party dependencies, plan and test continuity, manage incidents and maintain resilient information and communication technology.
- Intended chapter(s): 32, 34, 36, 47, 52 and 55
- Normative or interpretive: Official principles; Horizon Bank classifications are illustrative.

## Relevant summary

The Basel Committee presents operational resilience as an outcome that draws together governance, operational risk management, business continuity, dependency mapping, third-party management, incident management and resilient technology including cyber security. The principles are risk-based and should be applied with regard to a bank's nature, size, complexity and risk profile.

The book uses `impact tolerance` as an illustrative architecture field only when a jurisdiction or bank policy defines it. It does not invent numerical tolerances for Horizon Bank.

## Terminology and version notes
