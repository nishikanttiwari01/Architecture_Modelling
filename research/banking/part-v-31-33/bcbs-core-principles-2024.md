## Supported claims

- Claim: Prudential supervision considers capital adequacy, liquidity, large exposures, risk management, group structure and consolidated information.
- Claim: Capital requirements relate qualifying loss-absorbing resources to the risks undertaken by a bank.
- Claim: Banking groups and individual legal entities require explicit scope because risks and resources are not assumed to be freely interchangeable.
- Intended chapter(s): 32
- Normative or interpretive: The Core Principles are official. Horizon Bank's qualitative operating model and architecture consequences are author interpretations.

## Relevant summary

The 2024 Core Principles set minimum expectations for sound prudential regulation and supervision. Their assessment criteria cover governance and risk management as well as capital, liquidity, credit risk, large exposures and consolidated supervision.

Chapter 32 uses these principles to explain why capital, liquidity, legal-entity scope, positions, risk and reporting are separate architecture concerns. It does not assert that Horizon Bank is in a particular supervisory category or invent a jurisdiction's ratios and thresholds.

## Terminology and version notes

Use `capital adequacy`, `liquidity risk`, `banking group` and `legal entity` in their stated context. Do not present the Basel Framework as directly applicable without identifying the bank, jurisdiction and supervisory scope.
