## Relevant summary

The Basel Committee describes corporate governance as a foundation for robust and transparent risk management and decision-making. The guidance emphasises board oversight, senior-management responsibilities, risk governance, control functions and risk culture. The chapter uses those concerns to explain why architecture governance cannot be delegated to a repository tool or an information-technology team alone.

## Terminology and version notes

Do not present one committee structure as universal. Legal-entity and jurisdiction-specific governance remains outside the Horizon Bank baseline.
