## Supported claims

- Claim: Effective risk management depends on accurate, comprehensive and timely aggregation and reporting of risk data.
- Claim: Governance, architecture and infrastructure, aggregation capability, reporting and supervisory review are connected concerns.
- Claim: Reports should be reconciled and validated.
- Intended chapter(s): 32, 35, 36, 46, 47 and 55
- Normative or interpretive: Official principles; their use across Horizon Bank is an illustrative architecture recommendation.

## Relevant summary

BCBS 239 was issued after weaknesses in banks' ability to aggregate exposures and concentrations became evident during the financial crisis. It provides principles for governance, data architecture and infrastructure, risk data aggregation, reporting and supervisory review. Its original direct scope focuses on systemically important banks, while national supervisors may apply it more broadly. The book uses its ideas as a disciplined reference, not as a claim that every Horizon Bank entity is legally in scope.

## Terminology and version notes

Use `risk data aggregation and risk reporting`. Distinguish accuracy, completeness and timeliness. Avoid presenting the framework as a general-purpose data standard.
