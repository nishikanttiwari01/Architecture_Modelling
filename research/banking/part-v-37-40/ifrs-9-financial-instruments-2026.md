## Relevant summary

The IFRS Foundation describes IFRS 9 as governing classification and measurement of financial instruments and containing impairment requirements for expected credit losses. The standard's application requires detailed accounting policy, instrument classification, contractual cash-flow analysis and judgement. The chapters therefore trace business events to controlled accounting responsibilities without prescribing debit and credit entries for every product or jurisdiction.

## Terminology and version notes

Define expected credit loss (ECL) at first use. Do not imply that IFRS 9 applies to every Horizon Bank legal entity or that prudential credit-risk measures and accounting impairment are the same model.
