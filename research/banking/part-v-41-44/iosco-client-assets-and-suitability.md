## Supported claims

- Claim: Client-asset architecture must make ownership status, records, reconciliations and third-party custody responsibilities reviewable.
- Claim: Suitability for complex products depends on customer, product and jurisdictional context and requires retained evidence rather than a generic channel flag.
- Intended chapter(s): 44
- Normative or interpretive: International guidance; applicable legal duties for Horizon Bank are intentionally left pending.

## Relevant summary

The client-assets report gives regulators principles for intermediaries holding client assets, including clarity of status, records and responsibilities where assets are placed with third parties. The suitability report discusses customer classification, product complexity, assessment and oversight. Together they support the Horizon Bank separation of portfolio advice, investment orders, securities processing, custody positions and asset-servicing evidence.

## Terminology and version notes

Jurisdictions define client classes and detailed duties differently. The chapter therefore uses `private and institutional` as the Horizon Bank segment scope, not as a legal classification.
