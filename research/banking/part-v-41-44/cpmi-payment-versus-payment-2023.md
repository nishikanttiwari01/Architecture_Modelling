## Supported claims

- Claim: Foreign-exchange settlement risk can arise when one currency is delivered without receipt of the other.
- Claim: Payment versus payment links final settlement of one currency to final settlement of the other, while availability depends on currencies, instruments and arrangements.
- Intended chapter(s): 41
- Normative or interpretive: Official industry analysis; no particular mechanism is asserted for Horizon Bank.

## Relevant summary

The report explains the purpose and coverage limitations of payment-versus-payment arrangements. It supports an architecture distinction between quoting and booking a foreign-exchange deal, executing its two currency legs, monitoring settlement, and reconciling positions. A real bank still needs an arrangement-specific decision for currencies, legal entities, cut-offs and fallbacks.

## Terminology and version notes

Define foreign exchange as `FX` at first use. Define payment versus payment as `PvP` at first use and do not describe it as universally available.
