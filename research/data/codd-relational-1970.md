## Relevant summary

Codd's paper established the relational model as a way to represent data through relations and to reduce dependence on physical storage structures. The chapter uses this source to support the practical separation between logical data design and physical database implementation.

## Terminology and version notes

The book does not teach relational theory in depth. It uses the source to explain why tables, keys and constraints should not be confused with every conceptual business object.
