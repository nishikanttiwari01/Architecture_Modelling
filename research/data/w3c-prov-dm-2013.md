## Supported claims

- Claim: Data lineage and provenance can be explained through information about entities, activities and agents involved in producing or transforming data.
- Intended chapter(s): Chapter 8
- Normative or interpretive: Normative for W3C PROV terminology, interpretive for architecture lineage guidance.

## Relevant summary

PROV-DM defines a provenance data model for describing the people, activities and entities involved in producing data or other things. Chapter 8 uses it as a stable source for explaining why lineage is more than a data-flow arrow. A useful lineage view should show origin, transformation, responsibility and use.

## Terminology and version notes

The chapter does not teach full PROV notation. It uses the general provenance idea to explain architecture-level lineage and traceability.
