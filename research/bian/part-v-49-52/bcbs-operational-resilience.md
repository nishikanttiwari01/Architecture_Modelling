## Supported claims

- Claim: Operational resilience connects governance, operational risk management, business continuity, dependency mapping, third-party management, incident management and resilient information and communication technology.
- Claim: Resilience is assessed around delivery of critical operations through disruption, not only around duplicated infrastructure.
- Claim: A bank must set and approve its own applicability, impact tolerances and recovery objectives rather than copying invented values into an architecture diagram.
- Intended chapter(s): 52
- Normative or interpretive: Official principles; Horizon Bank classifications and scenario are fictional author models.

## Relevant summary

The Basel Committee presents operational resilience as an outcome supported by governance, dependency knowledge, continuity, incident response, cyber security and third-party management. Horizon Bank uses critical-operation and technology-classification records to make dependencies reviewable. It leaves quantitative impact tolerances, Recovery Time Objectives and Recovery Point Objectives pending until an accountable owner approves evidence-based values.
