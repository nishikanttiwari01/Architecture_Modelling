## Supported claims

- Claim: Network location or ownership alone should not grant implicit trust to a user, device, service or resource.
- Claim: Authentication and access authorisation are explicit concerns, and policy decision and enforcement responsibilities should be visible in architecture models.
- Claim: Zero trust is an architecture approach, not a product or a claim that segmentation is unnecessary.
- Intended chapter(s): 52
- Normative or interpretive: Official guidance for zero-trust concepts; interpretive for the Horizon Bank deployment and security model.

## Relevant summary

NIST SP 800-207 shifts the security focus from a static network perimeter to protected resources, identities and explicit access decisions. Chapter 52 uses this guidance to require authenticated service identities, policy enforcement and auditable access at important boundaries. It does not claim that Horizon Bank implements a particular United States government profile.

## Terminology and version notes

Define `Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)` at first use. Use access authorisation when the word could be confused with payment-provider authorisation or business approval.
