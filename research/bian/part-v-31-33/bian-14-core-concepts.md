## Supported claims

- Claim: BIAN arranges a peer set of Service Domains into the Service Landscape, with each Service Domain performing a specific business function or purpose.
- Claim: A Service Domain is a logical functional partition with associated Service Operations and lifecycle information concepts. It is not, by definition, one bank application, microservice or team.
- Claim: BIAN metamodel material includes Service Operations, Business Scenarios, Control Records, Behaviour Qualifiers and Business Object Model concepts.

## Relevant summary

The 14.0 digital repository presents the current BIAN metamodel and Service Domain records. The Service Domain material describes a logical responsibility within the peer set and connects it to Service Operations and information concepts. The repository also exposes Business Scenario, Control Record, Behaviour Qualifier and Business Object Model material.

The repository does not establish Horizon Bank's application, deployment, organisation or team boundaries. The book therefore maps BIAN objects to local responsibilities through qualified many-to-many relationships and records implementation decisions separately.
