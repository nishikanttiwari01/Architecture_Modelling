# Architecture Review Primary Sources

- **Topic:** Architecture review, stakeholder concerns, evidence and risk governance
- **Access date:** 2026-07-11
- **Source status:** Primary and official sources

## Sources

### ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2022

- Organisation: ISO, IEC and IEEE
- Title: *Systems and software engineering, Architecture description*
- Version: ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2022
- Use: grounds architecture-description, stakeholder, concern, viewpoint and view terminology. Chapter 28 applies these concepts to choosing review views. It does not claim that the standard prescribes the chapter's review workflow or outcome labels.
- Existing repository note: `research/fundamentals/iso-iec-ieee-42010-2022.md`.

### NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0

- Organisation: National Institute of Standards and Technology
- Title: *The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0*
- Version: 2.0, NIST CSWP 29
- Publication date: 26 February 2024
- URL: https://doi.org/10.6028/NIST.CSWP.29
- Use: supports governance, cybersecurity risk communication, prioritisation and treatment. It does not define a general architecture-review gate.
- Existing repository note: `research/security/nist-csf-2.0.md`.

### SEI quality-attribute scenarios

- Organisation: Carnegie Mellon University Software Engineering Institute
- Title: *Eliciting and Specifying Quality Attribute Requirements*
- Date: 2013
- URL: https://www.sei.cmu.edu/library/eliciting-and-specifying-quality-attribute-requirements/
- Use: supports measurable scenario questions for performance, resilience and other quality attributes.
- Existing repository note: `research/requirements/sei-quality-attribute-scenarios-2013.md`.

## Interpretation boundary

The review types, package guidance, record classification and outcomes of Proceed, Proceed with conditions, and Revise and return are author recommendations. They are not presented as ISO, NIST or SEI normative terminology.
